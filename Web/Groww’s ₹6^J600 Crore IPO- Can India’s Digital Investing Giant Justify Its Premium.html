Groww’s ₹6^J600 Crore IPO- Can India’s Digital Investing Giant Justify Its Premium

Aryaveer Batra
March 12, 2025  ·  6 min read

Groww (Billionbrains Garage Ventures Ltd) IPO: A Digital Finance Milestone

Billionbrains Garage Ventures Ltd, the parent company of investment platform Groww, has set the stage for one of the year's most anticipated listings, raising approximately ₹6,632.30 crore through a fresh issue and an offer for sale.

Founded in 2017 by former Flipkart executives Lalit Keshre, Harsh Jain, Ishan Bansal, and Neeraj Singh, Groww is a Direct-to-Customer (D2C) digital investment platform. Revenue is primarily generated from brokerage income and distribution commissions. It offers a variety of options, including stocks, derivatives, mutual funds, ETFs, and digital gold. They also provide Margin Traded Facility (MTF) and credit through their NBFC subsidiary. It has over 1.29 crore active NSE clients and a total customer base of over 1.7 crore users.

Structure of the Offer

  • A fresh issue of approximately 1,060 crores.
  • An offer for sale amounting to 5,572.30 crores. This is driven by shareholders and early investors, including Peak XV Partners, Ribbit Capital, and Y Combinator, who are selling only a portion of their investments. Notably, the company’s founders are selling a limited number of shares in this offering.
  • The company has set the price band between 95 and 100 per equity share. The company is valued at 61,736 crores at the upper end of the price band.

This issue is a 100% Book-Built Offer, and the IPO opens on November 4th, 2025, closes on November 7th, 2025, and lists on November 12th, 2025.

Key Financial Metrics

  • Revenue from operations surged to ₹3,901 crores in FY25, which is almost a 49% year-over-year increase.
  • The company reported a Profit after tax (PAT) of 1,824 crores, which is a dramatic reversal from an 805 crore loss in FY24 (which happened due to a one-time tax payment of 1,339 crores).
  • The adjusted EBITDA margin climbed to 59.11% in FY25, which is significantly higher than the global average. Furthermore, the company reported a Return on Net Worth of 37.57%.
  • As of 6th November 2025, the share is trading at a ₹13 Premium over the price.

Risks

  • Premium Valuation: The IPO is priced at a premium compared to competitors such as Angel One Ltd. and Motilal Oswal Financial Services Ltd. This places pressure on the company to maintain high growth to justify the valuation.
  • Competitive Landscape: The digital investment space is getting highly competitive. Failure to maintain a competitive edge could lead to customer loss and eroded margins.
  • Regulatory Environment: The business is subject to extensive regulatory requirements by SEBI and RBI. Any significant change in laws affecting derivatives or F&O could impact operations.

Market Outlook

According to analysts such as Anand Rathi Research and Bonanza, the company has strong fundamentals, market leadership, high user growth and retention, 12.6 million active clients, and strong profitability.

The post-IPO valuation is pegged at approximately 34x to 44x times its FY25 earnings, which is almost double that of its competitor, Angel One (20x-21x). This limits the potential for long-term financial gains. The company also relies heavily on brokerage income (approximately 80% of revenue). Diversification into lending and wealth management can be crucial for long-term growth.

Diversification Strategy

Groww is heavily reliant on its brokering service, which accounts for nearly 80% of the revenue. The brokering business is highly volatile due to market activity and regulatory changes, as the SEBI and the RBI closely monitor the F&O/derivative segment.

To mitigate this risk, the company is entering the wealth management and lending businesses. The primary objective of this diversification strategy is to reduce its dependence on the brokering business and to open new revenue streams. The long-term value of the company will depend on how it manages its new operations and maximises its value.
